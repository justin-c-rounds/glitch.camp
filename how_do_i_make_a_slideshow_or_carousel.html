How do I make a slideshow or carousel?

Warning: animating/transitioning images on a web page can be problematic. Be absolutely sure this is the right approach for your use-case and not just "eye-candy".

Generally speaking, automatically rotating slideshows (also known as "image carousels") are poor ways of communicating static visual information. Unlike a set of images, they don't let the user have control of what they are seeing. In addition, they impede contextual connections between images, which help reinforce the presented information.

Let's look at some approaches to do this based on the goals for your project.

Slideshow as Special Effect

This is the way to do this if we don't really care whether people look at the images or not, like a background image. In fact, we'll use background images in CSS to implement it.

  1. We'll start with a simple web page to prototype the slideshow:

              
            <!DOCTYPE html>
            <html>
              <head>
                <title>Simple Slideshow</title>
              </head>
              <body>
                <h1>Simple Slideshow<h1>
              </body>
            </html>

  2. Then we'll add a div element to contain our slideshow:
